## Cover note

Two things you asked for. I'll do them in order.

**What I built and led:** Over the weekend I built Wunder Radar — a multi-agent system that scans the global shared-mobility operator universe for switching signals and produces a weekly top-10 of operators most likely to re-platform in the next 6 months. The system scored 53 active operators against a Pain × Timing × Wunder Fit rubric. Top of the list this week: Europcar On Demand (2.1/5 app store rating, persistent post-rebrand), Cooltra Group (3 legacy stacks 18 months into the three-way merger), Nextbike (PE-acquired May 2024, rebrand window open). The brief, three deep-dive account plans, and the system itself are at [repo link] — Loom walkthrough here: [Loom link].

I built it because I think it's a representative slice of what the AI-Native GTM Associate would do in the first 90 days. I'd rather show that than describe it.

**Most useful AI workflow in the last 90 days:** A Claude-based pre-call research pipeline I run at TransferRoom. Football clubs are heterogeneous — different leagues, different financial regulations, different transfer windows, different decision-makers. Before each prospect call I run a multi-step Claude chain that ingests the club's recent transfer activity (TransferRoom internal data), their public financial situation (Companies House for English clubs, official filings for others), recent press, and the personality profile of the sporting director from interview transcripts. Output is a one-page brief in 90 seconds. It collapsed my pre-call prep from 45 minutes to 3, and I've started catching specific pain hypotheses I would have missed cold. The interesting failure mode: it over-weights the most recent press story. I now manually anchor the brief on the last 12 months, not the last 4 weeks.

About me: I'm an Account Executive at TransferRoom selling Core Platform (transfer marketplace) and Contingency AI (contract management) into clubs across five countries. I run a small advisory practice on the side helping pre-seed B2B SaaS founders with 0-1 GTM.

The Wunder role is specifically interesting to me because of the build — the explicit bet that AI-native and human GTM work together is the bet I'm already making in my day job. I'd rather be at the early stage of that thesis than the late stage.

## Notes on the framing (for anyone adapting this)

This is a reference template, not a script. If you adapt for your own application:

- Lead with the artifact (the work), not the bio. The reader is hiring a builder.
- Pick ONE secondary artifact ("Most useful AI workflow in last 90 days") that demonstrates ongoing capability, not just weekend-project capability.
- Close with the thesis match. Why this company, specifically. Not "I'm passionate about X."
- No "looking forward to hearing from you." It signals subservience and adds nothing.
- The "I'd rather be at the early stage of that thesis than the late stage" line is the most ambitious sentence. If it feels like overreach for your application, swap to: "I'd like 30 minutes to walk you through the brief in person."

## Things explicitly NOT included

- No "I'm passionate about mobility." (Nobody believes this from a cold note.)
- No formal greeting beyond first names. They use first names in the job ad.
- No mention of compensation expectations. (The salary band is in the JD; don't anchor lower.)
